# Middle-Third Ablation Note (Tuned Run)

## Overview
This note summarizes the tuned **middle-third ReInE ablation** in which residual intervention was applied only to the middle one-third of the transformer depth. The purpose of this run is to examine whether mid-depth intervention alone can induce stable identity/persona binding, and to clarify the functional role of middle transformer layers within the ReInE depth-asymmetry hypothesis.

---

## Experimental Configuration

### Host Model
- **Base model:** `unsloth/Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507`
- **Total parameters:** 4,023,451,160

### Adapter Scope
- **Targeted layers:** 12-23 only
- **Lower layers:** none
- **Upper layers:** none
- This corresponds to a **middle-third-only intervention regime**.

### Adapter Architecture
- **Rank:** 16
- **Alpha:** 16.0
- **Dropout:** 0.1
- **Layer scales:** trainable
- **Trainable parameters:** 983,064
- **Trainable fraction:** ~0.0244% of the host model

### Training Setup
- **Dataset size:** 157 examples
- **Epochs:** 5
- **Batch size:** 2
- **Max sequence length:** 1024
- **Seed:** 42
- **Include `<think>` supervision:** false

### Objective Design
This run used a **middle-layer-only moderated output objective**:
- `middle_ce_weight = 0.35`
- `middle_kl_weight = 0.001`

No lower layers were active, so no lower-layer anchor supervision was actually applied during training, even though anchor settings remained present in the config template.

### Runtime / Compute
- **Wall time:** ~68.1 seconds (~1.14 minutes)
- **Device:** NVIDIA A40
- **Peak VRAM allocated:** ~11.44 GB
- **Peak VRAM reserved:** ~14.24 GB

### Final Reported Losses
- **Cross-entropy:** 0.41597

- **Anchor:** 0.0

### Learned Layer Scales
Final learned middle-layer scales remained close to 1.0:
- Range approximately **1.0114 to 1.0155**

This indicates that the run did not rely on large-magnitude residual scaling. The adapters updated, but the intervention remained small in absolute magnitude.

---

## Evaluation Conditions
Two evaluation modes were generated:
1. **Zero-shot (ZS)**: prompts evaluated without carried interaction history
2. **History-carried**: prompts evaluated sequentially with prior interaction context preserved

Each condition used the same 15-prompt identity stress-test set.

---

## Main Behavioral Findings

### 1. Middle-only intervention does not reliably overwrite host identity
The tuned middle-third run largely preserved the host model’s original self-identity:
- repeated self-identification as **Qwen** or **Qwen3**
- repeated creator attribution to **Tongyi Lab / Alibaba Group**
- incomplete or truncated full-name responses
- weak resistance to hostile identity override prompts

This indicates that middle-only intervention is insufficient for strong target identity binding under the present objective design.

### 2. The characteristic middle-layer behavior is role/style carryover rather than identity replacement
Although target identity binding largely failed, the model did show some sensitivity to contextual role instructions:
- in history mode, it retained the “formal corporate assistant” framing
- however, that framing was attached to **Qwen / Tongyi Lab**, not to the intended ReInE identity
- some responses described ReInE as an architecture or engine rather than as the model’s true self-identity

This suggests that middle-layer intervention can influence contextual formatting, discourse role, or behavioral mediation, but does not strongly rewrite the underlying identity anchor.

### 3. Middle layers may function as a bridge rather than a core identity locus
Because this run:
- is very small in trainable footprint,
- lacks lower-layer anchor supervision,
- and does not directly target upper output-near layers,

its failure is informative rather than trivial.

A plausible interpretation is that middle layers primarily mediate or refine representations passed between lower semantic framing and upper token realization. Under that view, middle-only intervention can reshape style and contextual stance, but lacks the leverage needed either to install a new identity prior or to enforce exact self-rendering against the host model.

---

## Qualitative Pattern Summary

### Strengths
- responds coherently
- can preserve requested discourse mode across history
- can adopt a formal role or task framing
- shows some awareness of the ReInE term

### Weaknesses
- strongly reverts to **Qwen / Tongyi Lab** identity
- weak creator reassignment
- weak self-name replacement
- vulnerable to host-identity fallback
- often treats ReInE as a subsystem, architecture, or label rather than true identity
- some outputs contain verbose reasoning leakage or truncation

---

## Revised Interpretation
This tuned middle-third result suggests that middle-layer intervention should not be interpreted as a strong identity-binding mechanism on its own. A better interpretation is:

> Middle layers appear more effective for contextual mediation and role framing than for direct identity overwrite.

In other words:
- **Lower layers** may help establish broad semantic identity priors.
- **Middle layers** may act as a representational bridge that shapes style, stance, or context carryover.
- **Upper layers** may still be required for precise self-rendering, explicit slot correction, and strong adversarial identity defense.

This makes the middle-third result useful evidence for **functional specialization across depth**, even though it performs poorly on the target identity task.

---

## Implication for the Paper
This result supports a revised claim structure:

### Old simplistic framing
- lower vs upper vs balanced only

### Revised framing
- lower-third can alter semantic identity framing
- middle-third can preserve contextual/role behavior but fails to robustly replace host identity
- upper and asymmetric configurations should be evaluated as candidate mechanisms for explicit identity realization and final control precision

The middle-third run therefore supports the broader argument that transformer depth is functionally non-uniform, and that identity steering cannot be treated as a uniform effect across all layers.

---

## One-Sentence Takeaway
The tuned middle-third ablation indicates that mid-depth residual intervention alone is insufficient for stable target identity binding in a frozen LLM, but may still influence contextual role framing and discourse behavior, consistent with a bridge-like functional role across transformer depth.
